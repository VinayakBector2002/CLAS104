Module 15

Essential Element: Part III

Thyestes and Atreus

Thyestes therefore first becomes king at Mycenae, until Atreus later returns  and banishes Thyestes. Atreus also takes further revenge. He pretends at first to be reconciled with Thyestes and invites him back to Mycenae for a banquet in celebration. This, however, is a ruse. Atreus kills Thyestes’ sons (with the exception of one son, Aegisthus) and feeds them to him. Thyestes does not notice the gruesome nature of the banquet until he has eaten,  and curses Atreus (a natural reaction, surely!). Once again, we see the motif of cannibalism as well as the curse placed upon the family.
