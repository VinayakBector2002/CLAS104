Essential Element: Part I

Jason and the Argonauts

The Argonauts come from the generation before the Trojan war, and many of the fathers of the great Greek heroes of the Trojan war are counted among them. Our fullest account of the myth of Jason and the Argonauts’ quest to retrieve the Golden Fleece is from the third century B.C.E. by the poet Apollonius of Rhodes, but the myth certainly existed earlier. As Jason and his crew journey to the east and then back to the west, there are many overt parallels with the myth of Odysseus’ adventures, and it is probable that the two myths influenced each other throughout the centuries of their treatment in antiquity.

The number of the Argonauts (also known as the Minyae) varies in different accounts, but is around 50. We will obviously not be focusing on all of the Argonauts, but it is important to keep in mind that many of the Argonauts we have already encountered in other myths. The father of Achilles Peleus and Heracles, for example, make up part of Jason’s crew, as does the famous prophet and musician Orpheus.
