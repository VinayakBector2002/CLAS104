Essential Element: Part III

Return Home

When Perseus arrives back at Seriphos with Andromeda, his mother Danae and Dictys the fisherman have taken refuge at an altar (a sacred place in the ancient world where one gained the protection of the gods) because Polydectes was persecuting them. Perseus once again uses Medusa’s head to turn Polydectes and his followers to stone. He then returns the magical items to Hermes and gives Medusa’s head to Athena, who puts it in the middle of her shield. Perseus then returns to Argos with Andromeda and his mother Danae. Acrisius had heard that Perseus was still alive and fled to Larissa in Thessaly. Perseus follows him there and eventually kills him, although accidentally: while Perseus is competing in the games of the king of Larissa, he throws a discus which hits Acrisius. Once again we see the motif of a child, who is prophesied to bring harm to a family, exposed to die by a parent or grandparent, only in the end to survive and bring the prophecy to fulfilment. Perseus is, of course, also vindicated and recognized as a true hero at the end of his quest which is a recurring motif of folktale.
