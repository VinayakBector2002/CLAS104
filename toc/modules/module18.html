Module 18

Perseus

Objectives:

  • Look at the ancient quest narrative of Perseus and the slaying of Medusa.

Following from our examination of the journey of Odysseus, we are going to enter firmly into the world of folktale. Over the next few lessons we are going to look at a series of semi-divine heroes who embark upon quests and special missions. The structure of the quest in Classical mythology has had a profound impact upon literature and film up until the present day. One need, for example, look no further than Tolkien’s Lord of the Rings series or the well known Harry Potter books to find the motif and structures of the Classical quest echoed (Tolkien, for example, was himself an Oxford scholar of the Classics). We will see that these semi-divine heroes we examine in Greek and Roman mythology often perform a service for humanity (whether on a universal or individual level), make use of magic and special items to complete their journeys, and often go in search of items of particular importance. Today, we are going to look at a hero particularly connected with the Argolid in the Peloponnese, Perseus.
